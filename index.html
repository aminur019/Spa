Traditional Healing Massages

Traditional Thai Massage

Traditional Thai Massage, or Nuad Thai, is an ancient healing system from Thailand that blends pressure point therapy with deep stretching. Often described as "Thai yoga massage," the practitioner guides you through yoga-like stretches while applying rhythmic pressure. This technique combines elements of acupressure, Indian Ayurvedic principles, and assisted yoga postures, reflecting influences from neighboring India and China.

Historically, Thai massage is said to have been founded by Jivaka, the physician to the Buddha over 2,500 years ago. Today, it is recognized by UNESCO as intangible cultural heritage. Sessions are performed fully clothed on a mat without oils, using hands, elbows, knees, and feet to stimulate energy lines (Sen lines).

60 mins / $75 UNESCO Heritage Energy Work

Chinese Tui Na Massage

Chinese Tui Na (pronounced "twee nah") is one of the pillars of Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM). Its name, meaning "push and grasp," reflects the manual techniques used. Originating over 2,000 years ago, Tui Na has been used in Chinese hospitals to treat various conditions by balancing qi (vital energy) through meridians.

Techniques include brushing, kneading, pressing, rolling, and joint rotations resembling physical therapy. Unlike Swedish massage, Tui Na is more focused on specific ailments like back pain or neck stiffness. While some techniques may feel intense, the treatment improves circulation and harmonizes energy.

60 mins / $70 Pain Relief Meridian Therapy

Japanese Shiatsu

Japanese Shiatsu, meaning "finger pressure," evolved in early 20th century Japan from older practices like Anma. It applies steady pressure using thumbs, fingers, palms, and elbows to acupressure points and meridians, helping to alleviate stress, reduce muscle tension, and improve vitality.

Performed fully clothed on a mat or table without oils, Shiatsu's rhythmic pressure triggers the body's relaxation response. The calm, soothing environment enhances its meditative effect. Ideal for stress, back/neck pain, and headaches, it's a comfortable introduction to acupressure for all ages.

60 mins / $80 Stress Relief Non-Invasive

Indian Ayurvedic Massage (Abhyanga)

Ayurvedic Abhyanga is a cornerstone of India's 3,000-year-old healing system. The term means "massage" in Sanskrit and involves warm, herbal oils (often sesame) applied with long, flowing strokes to nourish body, mind, and spirit. It lubricates joints, enhances circulation, and supports lymphatic detoxification.

Typically performed by one or two therapists with light-to-moderate pressure, Abhyanga is deeply soothing. Post-massage, a steam or warm towel treatment helps oils penetrate. Clients report improved sleep, reduced anxiety, and glowing skin from this meditative practice.

75 mins / $90 Detoxifying Dosha-Specific

Korean Hand Therapy (KHT)

Developed in the 1970s by Dr. Tae-Woo Yoo, Korean Hand Therapy (KHT) is based on the concept that the hand mirrors the entire body. Therapists examine hands for sensitive points, then apply acupressure (sometimes with tools/magnets) to corresponding organs/body regions.

Sessions (30-60 mins) are done seated and can address headaches, sinus congestion, digestive issues, and joint pain. The non-invasive approach makes it ideal for those who prefer focused treatment without full-body exposure. Many report whole-body relaxation from hand stimulation alone.

45 mins / $65 Microcosm Theory Quick Relief

Indonesian/Balinese Massage

Balinese Massage blends Ayurveda, Chinese acupressure, and Indonesian traditions. Originating in Bali, it's known for luxurious spa experiences with warm coconut oil infused with local herbs (lemongrass, ylang-ylang). Therapists use long strokes, kneading, acupressure, and gentle stretching.

The rhythm alternates between deep tissue work and soothing strokes, inducing mental clarity alongside muscle relief. Ideal for stress relief and sensory rejuvenation, it's both therapeutic and indulgent—perfect for those seeking a tropical wellness escape.

90 mins / $110 Aromatic Oils Full-Body Relaxation

Tibetan Ku Nye Massage

Tibetan Ku Nye ("oil massage") balances the three humors (wind, bile, phlegm) through warmed herbal oils and manual techniques. Drawing from Ayurveda, Chinese medicine, and Tibetan practices, it alleviates stress, improves circulation, and restores harmony.

Sessions include rubbing, kneading, acupressure, stretching, and sometimes heat therapy (stones/compresses). A unique flour rub may conclude the treatment to exfoliate. Clients emerge with profound calm, relief from headaches/joint stiffness, and nourished skin.

80 mins / $95 Herbal Oils Energy Balancing
